The success equation

Why small improvements in decision quality produce outsized outcomes.

Every founder decision can be graded 0 to 1. Success is the product of all those decisions multiplied together — even modest, consistent improvement compounds dramatically.

Probability of Success = d1×d2×d3×···×dn

Because decisions multiply rather than add, a consistent improvement in quality produces a dramatically larger improvement in overall outcome probability.

Stage-matched peer groups

The stage system.

Members are organized into stage-pure rooms — four to eight founders facing the same kind of decision at the same time. Stage is how we make the room relevant; geography is just where it meets.

Stage 1

Finding the model

Cofounder and equity calls, first capital, first key hires, the search for product-market fit.

Stage 2

Building the machine

GTM model, the first repeatable sales motion, the early leadership team.

Stage 3

Scaling the organization

Senior and executive hires, the leadership layer, board dynamics, the first hard scaling decisions.

Stage 4

Operating at scale

Executive redesign, board pressure, market expansion — the decisions of a large, mature venture-scale company.
